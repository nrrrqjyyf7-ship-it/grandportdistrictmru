History of Grand Port

Grand Port, situated in the south-east of Mauritius, is one of the most historically significant regions of the island. It played a central role during Dutch, French, and British colonisation.

Dutch Settlement

The earliest European settlement in Mauritius began with the Dutch in 1638. They chose Grand Port because of its calm lagoon and favourable anchorage. The Dutch named the area Frederik Hendrik, in honour of Prince Frederik Hendrik of Orange, the ruler of the Netherlands at the time.
At Frederik Hendrik (present-day Vieux Grand Port), the Dutch established their first permanent settlement. They built fortifications, storehouses and simple dwellings. The settlement served as an administrative and military centre. The Dutch also introduced sugar cane, deer and ebony exploitation, which later played an important role in the island’s economy.
Despite its strategic importance, life at Frederik Hendrik was difficult. The settlers faced cyclones, droughts, food shortages and isolation. These hardships eventually led the Dutch to abandon Mauritius in 1710, leaving Frederik Hendrik deserted

French Period

In 1715, the French took possession of Mauritius and renamed it Île de France. Under French rule, Grand Port became a vital naval base and commercial harbour. The French developed the region by improving infrastructure, constructing roads, warehouses and sugar estates and moreover, strengthening coastal defenses

Grand Port played a major role in maritime trade and in protecting the island from enemy attacks especially during periods of conflict between European powers

Battle of Grand Port (1810)

This naval battle took place in the lagoon of Grand Port between the French and the British during the Napoleonic Wars. The French forces succeeded in defeating the British fleet, making it the only naval victory of France over Britain during this period.

This victory is immortalised on the Arc de Triomphe in Paris where the name “Grand Port” is engraved, highlighting the global importance of this event

Modern Times

Today, Grand Port is valued for its historical and cultural heritage. The Frederik Hendrik Museum at Vieux Grand Port stands as a reminder of the Dutch presence. The museum displays archaeological remains, cannons, tools and everyday objects from the Dutch and French periods
